# Contributors

OpenBallot Nigeria is built by a community of engineers, designers,
researchers, election observers, and civic-tech practitioners.

This file is regenerated from `git log` at every tagged release.
Between releases it carries the contributors recorded at the previous
tag.

## Founding scaffold

The initial scaffold (v0.1.0) was assembled by an automated build
under the direction of the founding consortium. The repository's
git history is the canonical record of who contributed what.

## How to be listed

Open a pull request. Once it lands you appear in the next release's
regenerated list. See `CONTRIBUTING.md` for the workflow.

## Acknowledgements

The platform's design takes inspiration from:

- The 2023 BBC analysis of Rivers state EC8As, which empirically
  established why a document-first multi-source architecture is
  necessary;
- IEC South Africa's results dashboard, as a baseline of what
  state-led aggregation looks like;
- The OpenStreetMap project, for the model of permissive geographic
  open data underlying our basemap layer;
- The Bitcoin and Ethereum communities, for the public-witness
  pattern we adapted as our audit anchor.

None of these projects endorses OpenBallot Nigeria; the lessons we
took are ours to answer for.
